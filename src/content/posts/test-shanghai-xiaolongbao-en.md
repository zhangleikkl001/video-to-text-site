Shanghai’s food culture has a long history, and its most iconic dish is undoubtedly xiaolongbao, or soup dumplings. Originating in Nanxiang, a town in Shanghai, these dumplings have a history of over 150 years. A thin wrapper encases a savory filling and rich broth, bursting with flavor when bitten into. From humble street stalls to chain stores now found worldwide, xiaolongbao has become a vital symbol of Chinese culinary culture. In recent years, as Chinese food brands accelerate their global expansion, more and more xiaolongbao brands have ventured abroad, opening branches in Japan, the United States, Europe, and beyond, winning the hearts of local diners. These brands not only preserve traditional techniques but also adapt to local tastes, creating innovative variations such as vegetarian xiaolongbao and seafood xiaolongbao.
