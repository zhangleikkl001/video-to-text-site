As the core of Macau's historic district, the Macau Peninsula offers a unique sense of ease and relaxation compared to the bustling Cotai with its cluster of five-star hotels. Here, you'll find the Ruins of St. Paul's, Senado Square, and many other historical sites, all while being close to the Zhuhai border for convenient travel and customs clearance.

![](/images/article/article-1779513596-fe0856/image_1.jpg)

Pic | Trip.com Community 
Today’s recommendation is the YOHO Treasure Island Hotel - Euro Lake. On a regular day, you can book a high-floor sea-view room for just three-digit prices, while also enjoying the convenience of a prime location on the Macau Peninsula.

Departing from Guangzhou South Station, a high-speed train ride of about 1 hour takes you to Zhuhai Station. After exiting the station, it’s a 5-minute walk to the Gongbei Port, followed by a roughly 10-minute sightseeing bus ride straight to the hotel. From Shenzhen, a high-speed train to Zhuhai Station takes just under 2 hours.

![](/images/article/article-1779513596-fe0856/image_2.jpg)

Image | Ctrip Community 
The hotel is located at No. 12 Hubin Road in the Nanwan District, with iconic views of the Macau Tower and Nam Van Lake right from the window. Plus, the doorstep is the largest bus interchange in Macau, making it super convenient for concert-goers.

Taking buses 26A, 33, or MT4 will get you directly to concert venues like The Venetian, Galaxy Arena, The Londoner, and Studio City. Most Macau buses run until around midnight, so after the show, you can hop on a shuttle bus straight back to the hotel.Image Credit: Trip.com Community

The hotel is close to many iconic attractions. Just a 5-minute walk away, you can reach Wynn Macau Hotel and enjoy the dazzling golden tree performance. As the sky dome displays koi fish forming the character "福" (fortune), the 21,000 crystal chandeliers on the ceiling slowly descend, complementing the golden tree adorned with 24K pure gold leaves. The scene is truly spectacular.

![](/images/article/article-1779513596-fe0856/image_5.jpg)

After watching the money tree performance, it's about a 10-minute walk to the Ruins of St. Paul's, where you can experience the elegance and historical charm of the ancient Baroque architecture.

![](/images/article/article-1779513596-fe0856/image_6.jpg)

Next to the Ruins of St. Paul's is the bustling and lively St. Paul's Handicraft Street, where many shops are century-old establishments. The wooden barrel charcoal-baked almond cookies from Cho Heng Bakery are very famous, using traditional charcoal baking to bring out the natural sweetness of almonds, soft and not sticky.

Along the street, you can also find thick-cut pork jerky, Portuguese egg tarts, pork chop buns, curry fish balls, and other specialties. Most of these foods, like peanut candy and almond cookies, come in gift boxes, making them perfect as souvenirs.

![](/images/article/article-1779513596-fe0856/image_7.jpg)

This hotel is also one of the rare family-friendly hotels in Macau. Right downstairs, you'll find the first Galeries Lafayette in Hong Kong and Macau, an outlet mall, a Pop Mart flagship store, and more—basically a one-stop shop for eating, drinking, and having fun.
