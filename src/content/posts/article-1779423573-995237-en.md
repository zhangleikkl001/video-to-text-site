Summer joy in Shanghai is always inseparable from playing with water! Here are five super-popular water attractions in Shanghai, offering you a variety of cooling strategies: indoor amusement parks, seaside beaches, forest-style swimming pools... Collect them now, so you won't get lost in summer water fun 🌊

**01  **
**Shanghai Maya Beach Water Park**
---
As the top outdoor water park in Shanghai, Maya Water Park is the first choice for thrilling summer water fun, with an atmosphere that's fully charged!

The park features a huge wave pool covering tens of thousands of square meters, rapid storm slides, super funnels, and dozens of other trendy projects. There are both thrilling high-altitude slides and child-friendly water play areas for families.

In summer, limited nighttime events will also be launched, including light shows, water-splashing parties, and NPC interactions. With the evening breeze and water play, the cooling atmosphere is perfect.

📍 Address: No. 1222 Linyin New Road, Sheshan Town, Songjiang District
⏰ Opening date: Starting June 14

**02  **
**Shanghai Yaoxue Water World**
---
Shanghai's rare year-round temperature-controlled indoor water world, unaffected by weather or season. You can enjoy water fun safely on rainy days and in scorching heat, focusing on comfort and convenience.

The venue is fully equipped with new facilities, including trendy and diverse play equipment like racing slides, lazy rivers, wading pools, and family water play areas, balancing excitement and relaxation.

The water is clear and temperature-controlled, the play areas are spacious and not crowded, saying goodbye to the annoyance of outdoor sun exposure. After cooling off in the water, you can also explore nearby ice and snow projects, unlocking a dual land-water experience in one stop. It's a treasure spot for water play in Shanghai.

📍 Address: No. 1-5, Lane 2088, Hucheng Ring Road, Nanhui New Town, Pudong New Area
⏰ Opening date: Starting May 30

**03  **
**Jinshan City Beach**
---
Jinshan City Beach offers a variety of activities: wading in the water, splashing waves, building sandcastles, and strolling along the beach, enjoying the seaside relaxation.

Water sports are exciting and fun, with options like jet skis, sailboats, water go-karts, and kayaks for a wind-riding experience. On land, you can ride beach buggies or go-karts, or have fun at the Alang Adventure Island family play area.

In the evening, watch the sunset along the coastal promenade, and the atmosphere is perfect. During peak season, there are also sand sculpture exhibitions, music fireworks festivals, and other events. It's a great place for summer leisure and family outings in Shanghai.

📍 Address: No. 7555 Huhang Highway, Jinshan District
⏰ Swimming period: June 20 - September 10, 8:30-21:30
⏰ Non-swimming period: March 1 - June 19, September 11 - November 30, 8:30-20:30; December 1 - February 28, 8:30-17:30

**04  **
**Fengxian Blue Sea and Golden Sand**
---
Fengxian Blue Sea and Golden Sand focuses on a leisurely and light water play experience, suitable for wading, walking, collecting shells on the beach, and playing in the sand with kids. There are no high-intensity thrill projects, emphasizing healing and relaxation.

The scenic area has wide views, a refreshing sea breeze, and is highly photogenic. Surrounding dining and rest facilities are complete.

During the day, wade and play in the blue sea and golden sand, embracing the coolness of summer. In the evening, chase an orange sunset at the Fisherman's Wharf and taste fresh seafood.

📍 Address: No. 6 Haihan Road, Fengxian District
⏰ Opening hours: 8:30-17:00 (swimming period is not yet announced)

**05  **
**Shanghai Sun Island**
---
A niche forest water paradise in Qingpu, hidden among thousands of acres of forest, away from the city crowds, focusing on a natural and tranquil water play experience!

Featuring an 18,000-square-meter large artificial beach swimming area with open waters and clean water quality. The shallow area is gentle and safe, making it the top choice for kids to play.

A 710-square-meter original ecological cave wonderland immerses you in the depths of a South American rainforest instantly, with the atmosphere instantly maxed out. A 3,000-square-meter huge wave pool lets you embrace layers of blue waves without traveling far to the seaside. Adults can have a blast, and children can play freely, taking over the whole summer's cool fun!

June weekend exclusive event: a romantic floating movie on the water. A huge LED screen floats on the blue waves; you can lie on the beach or soak in the water while watching a blockbuster. Summer coolness, a feast of light and shadow—this is what weekends should be like.

📍 Address: No. 2588 Shentai Road, Zhujiajiao Town, Qingpu District
⏰ Opening date: Starting May 29

**💡 Water Play Tips**

1. Be sure to apply sunscreen when playing outdoors, and prepare swimwear, waterproof bags, sun hats, and flip-flops.
2. There is a large temperature difference between day and night at the seaside beach, so bring a light jacket for the evening; some water park projects prohibit wearing accessories.
3. There is a large flow of people during holidays, so it is recommended to travel off-peak. Popular parks can be booked online in advance.
