Today marks the start of Xiaoman, or "Lesser Fullness," which signals rising temperatures and increased rainfall.

Beijing's weather today is quite fitting. According to the meteorological department, the sky will turn overcast this afternoon, accompanied by southerly gusts around level 5. A new round of rainfall will begin at night, with the heaviest period from late tonight to around noon tomorrow. The cumulative rainfall could reach moderate levels, with a relatively steady pace. The rain will make roads slippery and reduce visibility, negatively impacting tomorrow's morning rush hour. Please plan your travel in advance, bring rain gear, and drive safely.

Scattered showers or thunderstorms are still expected from tomorrow evening to the early night, so it's wise to keep rain gear handy and take timely precautions against lightning. Due to the rain, daytime temperatures will drop tomorrow, so sensitive individuals should add clothes to avoid catching a cold.

As of now, this weekend will see more cloud cover with rising temperatures, making it suitable for outdoor activities.

Looking ahead to next week, most of the time in Beijing will be sunny to partly cloudy, with temperatures trending upward. Highs will range from 26°C to 31°C, and lows from 16°C to 17°C. The temperature difference between day and night will be significant, with mornings and evenings feeling cool, so adjust your clothing accordingly. On the 25th (Monday) and 27th (Wednesday), weak cold air will bring scattered showers to some areas. Keep an eye on the latest forecasts and prepare in advance.
