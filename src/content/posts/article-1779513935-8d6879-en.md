**How to Play Hengqin Chimelong?**

Hengqin Chimelong has 2 theme parks, 2 shows, and 5 hotels.

It focuses on ocean and polar animals. Both theme parks, Ocean Kingdom and Spaceship Park, have a huge number of animals you can see up close: orcas, whale sharks, beluga whales, penguins...

**Spaceship Park**

Spaceship Park is an entirely indoor park with all kinds of marine life to see (the star orcas are here), plus large rides that are included in one ticket.

And since it's not affected by weather or heat, it's been incredibly popular since opening.

The first floor of Spaceship Park is the themed area, with the main draw being the various marine animals.

Besides orcas, you can also see Pacific white-sided dolphins, Baikal seals, West Indian manatees, hammerhead sharks, and other marine creatures and fish.

Even though it's an indoor park, the number of marine animals it houses is still impressive.

The second floor is the play area, also a crowd favorite. Its rides are generally much less intense than those in Ocean Kingdom.

If you usually can't handle roller coasters or pirate ships at amusement parks, you'll feel right at home in Spaceship Park!

Among the 4 parks, the Safari Park is the undisputed star.

Besides the sheer number of animals (over 20,000 little creatures live there), Chimelong is also really good at caring for them.

Chimelong's mascot, the white tiger, is also a point of pride. You can see white tigers, snow tigers, and golden tigers all in one place at Chimelong.

Currently, Qingyuan Chimelong has opened Chimelong Forest Kingdom, Chimelong Forest Hot Spring Paradise, and Chimelong Giraffe Castle Hotel. The hot spring paradise is open year-round.  
Chimelong Forest Kingdom  
The park in Qingyuan is huge, and the animals’ living environments are excellent, so many of them seem perfectly content there.  

Most of the animal habitats have no fences, putting them in the same space as visitors.  

Here, you can get up close to African lions, spot some monkeys chilling in hammocks, and check out the giant anteater breeding area and the place where you can see white-rumped langurs.  

A standard route through the park without backtracking goes: Parrot Rainforest → Bear Valley → Central Lawn → Bug World → Millennium Sacred Tree → Train Town (steam train crossing Africa) → Giraffe Prairie & Lion Mountain (Ferris wheel) → Monkey Island.  

But if you’re up for walking, I’d suggest heading straight to Train Town when the gates open. Riding the steam train across Africa is the hottest and most worthwhile experience at Qingyuan Chimelong—skipping it means you’ve missed out. After that, you can take it slow and explore in reverse.

Chimelong Forest Hot Spring Paradise  
Qingyuan is officially recognized by the Ministry of Land and Resources as the hometown of hot springs. Combined with Chimelong’s expertise in animal care, meerkats and hornbills have made their homes right by the hot springs, and the star attraction, Capybara, is also there. It’s a truly unique experience in China.  

You can even soak in the hot springs while watching animals up close—how could anyone resist that?!  

The hot spring quality is top-notch, too: the water comes from natural rock layers 2,300 meters underground. Qingyuan Chimelong has built over 40 themed pools, including a dazzling Virtual Shadow Pool that integrates VR technology, letting you watch movies while you soak.
