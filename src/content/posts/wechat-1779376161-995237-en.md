Summer in Shanghai isn't complete without water fun! Here are five top water play spots in Shanghai, offering a variety of ways to beat the heat: indoor water parks, sandy beaches, forest pools... Save this guide so you never miss the best spots for summer water fun 🌊


**01  **

**Shanghai Maya Beach Water Park __**

As Shanghai's most popular outdoor water park, Maya Water Park is the top choice for thrilling water fun in summer. The vibe is absolutely electric!

The park features a massive wave pool spanning tens of thousands of square meters, high-speed thrill slides like the Super Torpedo, the Giant Horn, and dozens of other popular attractions. It has both heart-pounding high-altitude slides and fun-filled family water play areas.

Summer also brings limited-time night events—light shows, water splashing parties, and NPC interactions. With the evening breeze and water fun, it's the perfect way to cool down.

📍 Address: 1222 Linyin New Road, Sheshan Town, Songjiang District
⏰ Opening Date: From June 14


**02  **

**Shanghai Yaoxue Water World __**

Shanghai's rare year-round indoor water world with constant temperature, free from weather and season restrictions. You can enjoy water fun safely even on rainy or extremely hot days, offering comfort and convenience.

The venue is fully equipped with brand-new facilities and trendy water attractions, including racing slides, lazy rivers, splash pads, and a dedicated children's area—catering to both thrill-seekers and those looking to relax.

The water is clear and temperature-controlled. Play areas are spacious and not crowded, so you can avoid the hassle of outdoor sun exposure. After cooling off, you can also head to the nearby ice and snow attractions for a one-stop land-and-water experience. It's a hidden gem for water fun in Shanghai.

📍 Address: Lane 1-5, 2088 Hucheng Ring Road, Nanhui New Town, Pudong New Area
⏰ Opening Date: From May 30


**03  **

**Jinshan City Beach**

Jinshan City Beach offers a variety of fun activities—you can splash in the water, ride the waves, build sandcastles, or take a relaxing stroll along the beach, fully enjoying the coastal charm.

Water sports are thrilling and fun—choose from jet skis, sailboats, water go-karts, kayaks, and more to experience the thrill of riding the waves. On land, you can drive beach buggies or go-karts, or let the kids have a blast at the Alang Adventure Island family play area.

In the evening, watch the sunset along the coastal promenade—the atmosphere is perfect. During peak season, there are sand sculpture exhibitions, music fireworks festivals, and other events. It's a great place for summer relaxation and family fun in Shanghai.

📍 Address: 7555 Huhang Highway, Jinshan District
⏰ Swimming Season: June 20 – September 10, 8:30-21:30
⏰ Non-Swimming Season: March 1 – June 19 & September 11 – November 30, 8:30-20:30; December 1 – February 28, 8:30-17:30


**04  **

**Fengxian Blue Sea & Golden Sand __**

Fengxian Blue Sea & Golden Sand focuses on a relaxing, light water play experience—perfect for walking in the waves, collecting seashells on the beach, and family sand play. No high-intensity thrill rides—just healing and relaxation.

The venue offers wide views and refreshing sea breezes, making it a great spot for photos. The surrounding area has complete dining and rest facilities.

During the day, splash in the blue water to embrace the coolness of summer. In the evening, chase an orange sunset at the Fisherman's Wharf and taste fresh seafood.

📍 Address: 6 Haihan Road, Fengxian District
⏰ Opening Hours: 8:30-17:00 (Swimming season dates not yet announced)


**05  **

**Shanghai Sun Island __**

A hidden forest water paradise in Qingpu, nestled in thousands of acres of forest, away from the city crowds. It offers a natural and tranquil water play experience!

The island features a 18,000 square meter artificial beach pool with open water and clean water. The shallow area is gentle and safe, making it an ideal water play spot for kids.

There is a 710 square meter ecological cave that instantly transports you to the depths of a South American rainforest—the atmosphere is incredible. A 3,000 square meter wave pool lets you embrace layer upon layer of blue waves without having to travel to the seaside. Adults can let loose, and children can play freely—all the summer coolness and joy are here!

June weekend exclusive: a floating movie on the water. A giant LED screen floats on the blue waves—you can lie on the beach or soak in the water while watching a blockbuster. Summer coolness and a feast of light and shadow—this is what weekends should be like.

📍 Address: 2588 Shentai Road, Zhujiajiao Town, Qingpu District
⏰ Opening Date: From May 29


**💡 Water Play Tips**

1. Be sure to apply sunscreen before outdoor water play. Bring swimwear, waterproof bags, sun hats, and slippers.
2. Temperature difference between day and night at the beach is significant; bring a light jacket for the evening. Some water park attractions prohibit wearing jewelry.
3. Holidays see large crowds; it's recommended to avoid peak times. For popular parks, book tickets online in advance.
