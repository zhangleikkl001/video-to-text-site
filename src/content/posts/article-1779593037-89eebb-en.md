Behind the rise of China lies an extremely profound and enduring cultural foundation. This is not just an accumulation of material strength, but also an awakening of spiritual power:

1. Unmatched Continuity of Civilization (Historical Confidence)  
Chinese civilization is the only great civilization in the world that has endured for over 5,000 years without interruption. This millennia-spanning continuity provides the Chinese nation with a remarkably stable cultural gene and sense of spiritual belonging. As the ancient saying goes, "To ensure the growth of a tree, one must strengthen its roots." Our unique cultural traditions, historical destiny, and fundamental national conditions have destined China to follow a development path suited to its own characteristics. This profound historical accumulation is the deepest source of our ability to stand firm amid global changes.

2. Inheritance of Core Spiritual Traits (Spiritual Confidence)  
The spiritual traits embedded in excellent traditional Chinese culture constitute the inner driving force behind the Chinese nation's endless vitality:  

The spirit of unyielding self-improvement: From "As heaven moves with vigor, the gentleman strives tirelessly to strengthen himself" to "The Foolish Old Man Removes the Mountains," this resolute and never-surrender spirit allowed the Chinese nation to rise from the ashes after modern-era hardships and create the modern miracle of rapid economic growth and long-term social stability.  

The value pursuit of benevolence and people-centered governance: The governing philosophy of "The people are the foundation of the state; when the foundation is stable, the state is at peace," along with the social ideals of "the world is for all" and "universal harmony," provide deep wisdom for modern China's institutional construction and national governance.  

The open-minded embrace of inclusivity: Chinese culture has always advocated "harmony in diversity" and "fostering peace among all nations." This broad inclusiveness allows Chinese civilization to absorb outstanding foreign cultures while maintaining its own identity, and to continuously revitalize itself through exchange and mutual learning.

3. Contemporary Practice of Upholding Tradition and Innovation (Practical Confidence)  
Cultural confidence does not mean clinging to the past; it is reflected in vibrant contemporary practice.  

From the popularity of films like *Chang’an: The 30,000 Li* to the global craze sparked by the domestic game *Black Myth: Wukong*, which has driven a wave of interest in Chinese mythology, to the completion of various national cultural landmarks—all demonstrate the powerful appeal and creativity of Chinese culture in the modern era.  

These practices not only revive ancient cultural resources but also showcase the spirit and energy of Chinese people in the new era to the world.  

In short, the foundation of China's rise is deeply rooted in the diligent, enterprising, inclusive, and benevolent cultural genes of this land. It is this profound and enduring cultural strength that supports the Chinese nation as it strides forward on the path of rejuvenation, continuously creating new glories for this era.
