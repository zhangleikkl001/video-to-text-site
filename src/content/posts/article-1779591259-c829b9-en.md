**Jingmei Village, Yamen Town, Xinhui District**

Jingmei Village, located in the northwest of Yamen Town, covers an area of 3.7 square kilometers and is 1.75 kilometers from the town center. According to legend, during the Yuan Dynasty's Zhizheng era, ancestors with the surname Chen settled here and named it "Gengbei Chen Village." During the Ming Dynasty's Zhengde era, a clansman passed the imperial exam and established a new village, taking the meaning of "plum blossoms bloom every year in the capital" and renaming it Jingmei.

![](/images/article/article-1779591259-c829b9/image_11.jpg)

Jingmei Village is rich in tourism resources, featuring the nationally protected first-class Hongsheng Ancestor Hall, as well as historical buildings such as Chen Xiang's former residence and Kangwang Temple, which are listed in the Xinhui District's immovable cultural relics register.

Photographed by Peng Weizong

Here, the must-mention is the "Shen Chu Hua Xiang" alley, where the art of painting blends into the passage. Hidden along the alley are vivid, lifelike murals showcasing martial arts secrets—musical notations, ten core techniques, weaponry use, and even life-size models for you to interact with and try out a move. By the time you walk out of the alley, it feels as if you've absorbed the essence of Choy Li Fut boxing, like a dragon or tiger possessing you, itching to take action. Besides uncovering martial arts secrets, you can also visit the Hong Sheng Ancestral Hall, the birthplace of Choy Li Fut, and the Kang Wang Temple to marvel at the ancient, charming historic architecture and soak in its profound cultural heritage.

**Caoping Village, Shuibu Town, Taishan City**

Caoping Village is located in Changtang Village, Shuibu Town, Taishan City. It was established during the Daoguang reign of the Qing Dynasty, over 160 years ago. The village still preserves intact the founding stone tablet, ancestral hall, and school from that era. The people of Caoping place great importance on education, with existing old-style private schools like the Longshen Family School built during the Qing Dynasty and the Caoping Primary School built in 1930. The village boasts many notable figures, such as Lin Yuanfang, a high-ranking official in the Ministry of Revenue appointed by the Daoguang Emperor of the Qing Dynasty.**
The grassland village is rich in the culture of overseas Chinese hometown and has abundant resources. By introducing a cultural tourism planning company, it pioneered the "village shareholder system" and promoted the revitalization project of the grassland village. So far, environmental improvements, tourist restrooms, the renovation of the grassland primary school, a coffee school, the grassland square, sightseeing plank roads, stone-paved main streets, partial facade restoration of some ancient houses, and lighting projects have been completed. Observation decks, waterfront platforms, and the Lintang Garden project are also set to be finished soon.

Night view of Caoping Village, photographed by Peng Weizong.

In addition, projects such as an agricultural museum, a hydrophilic wetland park, a cultural arts center, an artist creative camp, an RV camp, a century-old overseas Chinese hotel, a paddy field experience inn, water recreation facilities, and a happy vegetable garden have been included in the plans. The Caoping Village cultural tourism project integrates rural scenery, organic agriculture, specialty dining, overseas Chinese culture, and trendy guesthouses, blending advanced tourism concepts to strive to become a model benchmark for rural cultural tourism projects in Taishan and even Jiangmen.

**Wufeng Village, Haiyan Town, Taishan City**

Wufeng Village is an administrative village under Haiyan Town, located in the southwestern part of Taishan City. It borders Jilong Mountain to the east and faces the South China Sea to the south, with a view of Shangchuan and Xiachuan Islands across the sea. It is a place where you can see mountains, gaze at the sea, and remember your nostalgia.

Video 
Wufeng Village is small, covering only 1.9 square kilometers, yet it is also "big," known as a "mini United Nations." Originally built in 1962 as a resettlement village for overseas Chinese evacuated from Southeast Asia, it is home to returned overseas Chinese from 13 countries and regions in Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, Vietnam, Myanmar, India, Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, Thailand, Singapore, the Philippines, Brunei, and Sri Lanka.

In Wufeng Village, you can experience authentic Southeast Asian culture; taste unique Southeast Asian cuisine such as Vietnamese spring rolls, Malaysian fried chicken, Indonesian braised crab, and Indonesian pandan rolls; and find satisfying ethnic costumes, handicrafts, and fruits from various countries.

**Qiangya Village, Tangkou Town, Kaiping City**

Qiangya Village Committee is located in the central part of Tangkou Town, Kaiping City, west of Tangkou Market. The village has a total of 566 households with a population of 1,751, governing 9 village groups: Shuilong Village, Shangtang Second Village, Shangtang First Village, Guangling Village, Zili Village, Qiling Village, Zuzhai Village, Miaobei Village, and Miaodong Village.
Among them, Zili Village, under the jurisdiction of Qiangya Village Committee, is a World Cultural Heritage site and a nationally renowned historical and cultural village. The Zili Village Diaolou cluster was designated as a National Key Cultural Relic Protection Unit by the State Council in June 2001.

Zili Village features 15 diaolou with diverse styles, exquisite designs, and rich cultural significance, standing as outstanding representatives of the flourishing period of Kaiping Diaolou. Most of these diaolou were built in the 1920s and 1930s by local overseas Chinese to protect the lives and property of their families and fellow villagers back home.The strong in the image

Qiangya Village Committee of Tangkou Town fully leverages the brand effect of the Zili Village scenic area, vigorously promotes the adjustment of the agricultural industry structure, and plants crops such as rice, rapeseed flowers, and lotus roots (lotus flowers), attracting a large number of visitors to enjoy the rural scenery and driving the development of rural tourism economy.
Recently, the Tangkou Town ancestral home tourist restroom, which won the 2020 World Landscape Architecture Award (WLA) for Best Small Architecture (Built Small category), has become a true internet-famous check-in spot, drawing a steady stream of visitors every day.Zuzhai Village belongs to Qiangya Village Committee, Tangkou Town, and is adjacent to the world cultural heritage site—Zili Village. It is the ancestral home of the Fang clan in Tangkou. The main cultural relics and historical sites in the village include the immovable cultural relics: Suicheng Tower, Wenyuan, Wendiao Tower, and Xiancheng Tower.
