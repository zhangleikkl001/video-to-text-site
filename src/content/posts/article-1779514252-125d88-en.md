Back to the old question: how to choose among the 7 hotels?

Before Senbo came along, family weekend vacations meant either a "day trip" to an amusement park, where adults were exhausted after keeping the kids entertained all day, or staying at a scenic resort hotel where kids had almost nothing to do except the pool. Kaiyuan Senbo offered the solution of "stay and play to the fullest."

Over seven years, across seven cities, every Senbo is not a simple copy.

Here's a big task: an in-depth comparison of the 7 Kaiyuan Senbo resorts.

[Hangzhou Senbo] is the first Senbo in China. As the "prototype," Hangzhou Senbo may not be the largest in scale, but it excels in balance and maturity, plus its location just 30 minutes from downtown Hangzhou gives it a super high repurchase rate.

Rooms are divided into two sections: the main building and the vacation homes. The main building has 172 rooms, right next to the Senbo Park, perfect for families seeking convenience; the 243 vacation homes scattered across wetlands, forests, and lakesides offer another option for guests seeking privacy and immersion.

The Water Park covers about 25,000m² indoors and outdoors. Though it's been surpassed by later resorts, it's still not small. With over 30 attractions like wave pools, water forts, kids' pools, climbing pools, and sports pools, the most notable is China's first rapid river, 256 meters long, circling the park. These have become standard for later Senbo resorts.

During the peak summer season, the Water Park also hosts water parties, light shows, hula dances, and fire shows.

Fantasy Island covers over 3,000m² with a ceiling height of nearly 9 meters. It was last upgraded in 2024 and is highly playable, with indoor go-karts, ball pits, Lego labs, simulated kitchens, and princess dress-up rooms, covering fun for all ages.

The Top Player arcade is about 2,000m², combining global trendy tech like AR and VR, so adults can also relive childhood joys.

Besides the flagship Water Park and Fantasy Island, there's also a petting zoo. BoBo Friends Circle lets you interact up close with sika deer, groundhogs, white kangaroos, raccoons, Shetland ponies, alpacas, peacocks, and more. Feed needs to be purchased separately (about 20 yuan per portion).

There's also a Senchi go-kart track in the park plaza.

The first generation was meant to be surpassed, but Hangzhou Senbo set high standards and is still in its prime, making it the top choice for weekend getaways in the surrounding area.

[Moganshan Senbo] is the largest in scale, covering 1,800 acres, six times the size of Hangzhou Senbo! Even in the entire Yangtze River Delta resort market, this scale is enough to stand out.

The biggest headache at Moganshan Senbo might be that three days and two nights aren't enough to experience everything.

With 5 major outdoor areas, a 28,000m² Water Park, and a 4,200m² Fantasy Island, there are over 100 play facilities.

The 300-meter-long Lazy River in the Water Park is the most popular. Among the thrilling attractions, don't miss the Atlantis-style "Mermaid Slide" and "Laser Tunnel," while underwater world snorkeling is an innovative feature of Moganshan Senbo.

Fantasy Island is the largest except for Wuhan Senbo, and it just reopened in April this year with newer and more complete facilities.

Moganshan Senbo also has outdoor advantages. Leveraging the quarry terrain, lakes, and forest resources within the 1,800-acre estate, they've developed 5 major outdoor play areas, including over 10 large outdoor attractions.

The petting zoo is another highlight. On Elf Island, you can interact up close with raccoons (Senbo even bred triplets independently); on Youyou Tribe, you can feed sika deer; and Bobo Backyard is a mini-farm with alpacas, rabbits, mini pigs, and Shetland ponies.

Moganshan Senbo's accommodation system is also the largest in the Senbo series, with over 1,100 rooms divided into four sections, covering everything from budget to luxury.

The main building is the entry-level option, with rooms 35-38m², biggest advantage being convenience. Elevators take you straight to the restaurant, Water Park, Fantasy Island, and Flora Garden, just a 3-5 minute walk.

Wowawa Kingdom Hotel is the "happiness amplifier" for families. The entire hotel is a giant playground, with a huge slide and climbing net in the lobby, and corridors and doors full of fairy-tale charm.

The wooden cabins are the soul of a Moganshan vacation. Independent cabins are scattered across lakesides, valleys, fields, and tea gardens, from 31m² basic cabins to 351m² mountain-view two-story five-bedroom pool villas, with over 20 room options.

The Yinzhuli luxury area represents Senbo brand's extension into luxury vacations, hidden deep in the bamboo forest valley, with each room equipped with a dedicated butler and 24-hour dedicated shuttle.

[Zhoushan Senbo] is Kaiyuan Senbo's first seaside resort complex. What everyone looks forward to most at a seaside hotel are sea-view rooms. Over 70% of the rooms at Zhoushan Senbo have sea views, starting from 47m², offering plenty of space. It's also been rated by many guests as "one of the best spots in Zhujiajian to watch the sunset."

In terms of room design, Zhoushan Senbo has four styles: the 3rd floor is an ocean-themed family floor with slide beds, kids' tents, and adjustable sinks; floors 5-8 feature a wabi-sabi style with simple, natural tones and sea-view balconies, a favorite photo-ready style for young people; plus Mediterranean and Moroccan styles.

The Water Park is similar in size to Moganshan Senbo's, and the outdoor Water Park open in summer truly highlights the seaside advantage.

Fantasy Island has an ocean theme, with 1,600m² not as impressive as other "giants," but it still meets the play needs of kids aged 2-10.

Top Player is more suitable for older kids, mainly combining trendy tech like AR and VR with exciting interactive games like flight fighters and interstellar racing, offering over 40 attractions.

Moreover, Zhoushan Senbo's park has an extra Top Player option, which includes single

1 Go-Kart Ride + 1 Individual Interactive Cinema + 50 Game Tokens.

The entertainment options here are the most complete I've seen in any Senbo property. The gym has mountain bikes, water rowing machines, fitness mirrors, and indoor ski simulators. There’s also a karaoke room, a party house, and a billiards room—hitting the social needs of parents and young people spot on.

On the 17th floor, the all-floor-to-ceiling-window Starry Lounge offers a 270-degree high-altitude view of the mountains and sea, with an infinity pool that blends into the ocean, perfect for watching the sunset paint the sky and water.

The hotel is only a 10-15 minute drive from the Wugongzhi Pier, and from there, it’s about a 15-minute boat ride to Putuo Mountain. This means guests can easily combine a trip to the amusement park with Putuo. Daqing Mountain Scenic Area (about 1.6km), Nansha Scenic Area (about 2.44km), and Dongsha Bay Beach are all within a few minutes’ drive. Around the hotel, you’ll also find local food spots like Shenjiamen Night Snack Street and the Gaojiazhuang Seafood Restaurant.

【Jiaxing Senbo】 is the first Senbo with hot springs, and it also has the largest water park among the Jiangsu-Zhejiang-Shanghai Senbo locations.

The water park and Fantasy Island are still the usual strong suits, and this one is pretty new.

Let me focus on the hot springs. The 9,600㎡ Xihe Hot Spring Center has both indoor and outdoor pools. Indoors, there are carbonated spring pools and a constant-temperature swimming pool; outdoors, you can soak while enjoying the mountain views of South-North Lake. Plus, there’s a sauna room and a full SPA experience. The Xihe Hot Springs and the water park open alternately, with the hot springs operating roughly from November to February.

The hotel is in Haiyan, Jiaxing, right next to the South-North Lake Scenic Area—just out the back door and you’re there. The setting sounds amazing, especially since South-North Lake is nicknamed "Little West Lake."

Besides that, if you come in autumn, you can pick fruit from hundreds of orange groves.

There are two musical fountain light shows at 7 PM and 8 PM every night, which is something other places don’t have, and hotel guests can watch for free.

【Tonglu Senbo】 marks the official entry of the New Century Senbo brand into the 2.0 era. The total planned area is 3 square kilometers, but only the first phase—Wai Po Jia Resort Hotel—opened last July.

You might have noticed that Tonglu Senbo lacks a water park and Fantasy Island. For now, it has an indoor super-long swimming pool, with the Seventeen-Changchi Mountaintop Hot Springs on the upper level, where seventeen pools are scattered naturally.

In comparison, Tonglu Senbo turns nature into a playground. There are ancient hiking trails, riverside fishing, ATV off-roading, the Bamboo Addiction Bar by the ancient ferry on the Fuchun River, and a café in a natural cave—all clearly aimed at couples and younger crowds.

Another highlight is the 2,500㎡ riverside non-powered playground along the Fuchun River. Kids can run and climb on the big riverside lawn, playing in nature, which fits the 2.0 playground philosophy.

Something to look forward to: the second phase is progressing quickly and will include a water park and an upgraded indoor children’s play area.

Tonglu Senbo takes accommodation to the next level by making it architectural art that naturally grows from the landscape. The Conch House uses a raised design hidden in the woods, with its spiral shape echoing nature’s golden ratio. The rooms have hidden spiral slides that let kids slide straight into a "secret space" inside the conch.

The Carbon Crystal Cottage is completely pitch-black, with its design inspired by traditional carbon carving, blending the house into Tonglu’s mountains and forests. The Starry Sky Room lets you gaze at the Milky Way through a panoramic skylight.

The Tree Nest includes six independent rooms, each with windows opening to greenery, perfect for family or friend groups. There’s also the main building, Huaishan Pavilion, and the Yujiang Cottage with its own private courtyard.

【Rizhao Senbo】 wins over the northern market with its scale-leading water park.

The water park covers over 50,000 square meters and can accommodate 5,100 visitors at once. The indoor section is 20,800㎡, with all the standard features like a rapids river, surfboard riding, an infinity pool, and a water fortress. The snorkeling pool is unique to Rizhao, and the Time Travel slide set of five is widely considered the most fun in the park.

The outdoor water park is about 30,000㎡, with sun, sand, sea, and many large play structures—though the outdoor season is shorter in the north, running roughly from June to August.

Fantasy Island is average in size, but its ocean theme is still a big draw.

You can’t come to Rizhao without beachcombing. From the hotel’s second floor, you can go straight to Gate 3 of the Wanpingkou Coastal Scenic Area, and a three-minute walk lets you step on the sand and chase the waves. It’s also a prime spot to watch the sunrise over the sea. The hotel even provides free beachcombing tools, so taking the kids to dig for crabs and shells at low tide is a natural experience that indoor playgrounds can’t replace.

In terms of accommodations, Rizhao Senbo is very generous: basic rooms in the main building start at 45㎡, and the sea-view rooms on floors 5-6 have great views. There are also vacation cottages and only six Tree Nest treehouses.

【Wuhan Senbo】 literally expands the size of Fantasy Island several times over. Indoor and outdoor combined, it spans 12,000㎡, more than all the previous ones put together.

The outdoor castle alone is five stories high, and indoors you can even drive a small train. Probably because Wuhan is known as a furnace, playing indoors in the summer is key.

Head Player still goes for a cyberpunk vibe, with holographic projection streets and top-tier VR equipment, plus over 30 games.

There’s also a 7D flight cinema with a curved screen for an immersive adventure experience.

Meanwhile, the water park is no slouch either, at 28,000㎡, matching the size of the one at Mogan Mountain Senbo.

Main building rooms start at 38㎡, each with a balcony or a water-facing deck, and a dedicated passageway directly to the amusement park, so you can head down and start playing.

The standout room types here are the artificial banyan tree houses and the boat houses by the lake, which are often fully booked.

Wuhan Senbo is located on a peninsula surrounded by water on three sides at Houguan Lake, which is also the largest wetland park in Wuhan. Leveraging the surrounding environment, the hotel has designed a fantastic scenic route around the lake, doubling as an open nature education class. Plus, kayaking, a petting zoo, a fireworks show every Saturday night, and bubble parties further enrich the all-day experience of "playing in the water during the day and watching shows at night."
