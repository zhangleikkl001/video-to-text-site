Let me introduce Shanghai Haichang Ocean Park to newcomers.

Haichang Ocean Park started in Dalian and has nearly 30 locations nationwide. Shanghai Haichang is the flagship, with a total investment of 5 billion yuan and covering about 550,000 square meters. It combines animal exhibits, themed entertainment, cultural leisure, science education, and spectacular performances.

In plain terms, it's both an ocean park and a combo of amusement park and performance venue—one ticket covers a ton of fun.

Recommended route: Time and Space Cable Car – Volcano Rafting – Volcano Shark Pavilion – Underwater World Pavilion – Coral Jellyfish Pavilion – Ultraman Theme Pavilion – Iceberg Arctic Pavilion – Antarctic Penguin Pavilion – Sea Creature Exploration Pavilion. This way, you won't backtrack too much.

I'd break down Shanghai Haichang Ocean Park into several parts:

1) Marine Animal Viewing

Shanghai Haichang houses over 100,000 marine animals, with stars like orcas, beluga whales, dolphins, polar bears, and various penguins... Just among penguins, there are three species: emperor, Adélie, and gentoo. The penguin pavilion also has a rare two-story "penguin super bowl" where you can see penguins swimming overhead.

I can responsibly say that among all ocean parks in East China, none have more animals than Shanghai Haichang.

2) Watching Shows

Shanghai Haichang has 10 major shows. For example, "Orca Legend" is a must-see—Shanghai Haichang is the only place in Jiangsu, Zhejiang, and Shanghai to see orcas, and you can watch up to six perform together.

"Dolphin Encounters" features nine dolphins jumping in unison for a dreamy performance.

"Sea Lion Comedy Show" stars a famous sea lion nicknamed "Little Qian..."

And the mermaid underwater ballet performance "Mermaid Tale" is stunningly beautiful.

Anyway, last time I went to Shanghai Haichang, I couldn't finish watching all 10 shows in one day—there were just too many, too busy!

3) Ride the amusement attractions

Shanghai Haichang also has Dolphin Roller Coaster, Volcano Rafting, Time-Space Cable Car, Dome Theater, 4D Theater, and over a dozen other large rides. Except for the cable car and the Ice Theater, everything else is included with a single ticket, no extra payment needed.

Highly recommend the Dolphin Roller Coaster, which runs through most of the park. My experience was that it throws all sorts of continuous turns and backflips at you—the feeling of losing control is thrilling but also so much fun.

Volcano Rafting, on the other hand, combines rapids and a bowl-shaped pool, tackling both excitement and splashes. It's a must-play in summer.
